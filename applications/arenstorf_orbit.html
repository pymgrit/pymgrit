Arenstorf orbitÂ¶

  • source: arenstorf_orbit.py

  • example code: example_arenstorf.py

  • system of two scalar second-order ODEs with two unknown functions solved as system of four scalar first-order ODEs

  • discretizations: RK45 from scipy.integrate

Arenstorf orbit is a special case of the three body problem from Astronomy, where two bodies of masses \(\mu\) and \(1-\mu\) (e.g., the moon and the earth) are orbiting in a plane, along with a third body of negligible mass relative to the other bodies (e.g., a satellite) in the same plane. The equations are

\[\begin{split}x'' &= x + 2y' - (1 - \mu)\frac{x + \mu}{D_1} - \mu\frac{x - (1-\mu)}{D_2},\\ y'' &= y - 2x' - (1-\mu)\frac{y}{D_1} - \mu\frac{y}{D_2},\end{split}\]

where \(D_1\) and \(D_2\) are functions of \(x\) and \(y\),

\[D_1 = ((x + \mu)^2 + y^2)^{3/2}, \;\;\; D_2 = ((x - (1-\mu))^2 + y^2)^{3/2}.\]

Here, the earth is at the origin and the moon is initially at (0,1). The mass of the moon is \(\mu = 0.012277471\) and the mass of the earth is \(1-\mu\).

If the initial conditions are chosen to be

\[x(0) = 0.994, \;\;\; x'(0) = 0, \;\;\; y(0) = 0, \;\;\; y'(0) = -2.00158510637908252240537862224,\]

then the solution is periodic with period \(T = 17.0652165601579625588917206249\). This example is taken from 1.
